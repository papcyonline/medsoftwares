## Understanding EMR vs EHR

Before choosing software, understand the terminology:

### EMR (Electronic Medical Records)
- Digital version of paper charts
- Used within single practice
- Contains medical and treatment history
- Practice-specific records

### EHR (Electronic Health Records)
- Broader view of patient care
- Shareable across providers
- More comprehensive scope
- Interoperability focused

For small clinics, these terms are often used interchangeably. Focus on features rather than labels.

## Pricing Models Explained

### Subscription (Per Provider/Month)
- Most common model
- $100-500 per provider monthly
- Ongoing cost forever
- Features often tiered

### Per-Transaction
- Pay per claim or encounter
- Variable monthly costs
- Can become expensive
- Hard to budget

### One-Time Purchase
- Single payment
