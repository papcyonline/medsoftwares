## Shortage Management Best Practices

### Prevention Strategies

**1. Diversify Suppliers**
- Never rely on single source
- Establish backup relationships
- Compare pricing regularly
- Track performance

**2. Monitor Industry News**
- FDA shortage announcements
- Manufacturer communications
- Industry publications
- Professional networks

**3. Optimize Inventory**
- ABC classification for priorities
- Safety stock for critical items
- Regular cycle counting
- Expiry management

**4. Forecast Demand**
- Historical usage analysis
- Seasonal patterns
- Protocol changes
- External factors

### Response Strategies

**When Shortage Occurs:**

1. **Assess Impact**
   - Current stock level
   - Daily consumption rate
   - Days of supply remaining
   - Patient impact assessment

2. **Identify Alternatives**
   - Therapeutic equivalents
   - Dosage form changes
   - Treatment protocol modifications
   - Manufacturer alternatives

3. **Communicate**
   - Notify clinical staff
   - Update protocols
   - Inform patients
   - Document decisions

4. **Conserve Supply**
   - Restrict to essential use
   - Review all orders
   - Prevent waste
   - Consider dose optimization

5. **Source Alternatives**
   - Contact other suppliers
   - Check regional availability
   - Consider compounding
   - Emergency purchases

### Recovery Strategies

**When Stock Returns:**

1. **Verify Supply Stability**
   - Confirm sustainable supply
   - Check for ongoing issues
   - Verify pricing

2. **Restore Normal Operations**
   - Return to standard protocols
   - Update formulary status
   - Communicate to staff

3. **Learn and Improve**
   - Document experience
   - Update safety stocks
   - Revise supplier strategy
   - Improve forecasting
