Laboratory services are the backbone of modern healthcare, with 70% of clinical decisions based on lab results. A robust Laboratory Information System (LIS) is essential for accuracy, efficiency, and compliance in clinical diagnostics.

![Laboratory Information System Market 2026](/infographics/lis-lab-system-market-2026.png)

## What is a Laboratory Information System (LIS)?

A Laboratory Information System is specialized software designed to manage clinical laboratory operations. It handles everything from test ordering to result reporting, ensuring accurate, timely diagnostic information.

### Core Functions

- **Order Management**: Receive and process test requests
- **Specimen Tracking**: Monitor samples through processing
- **Result Entry**: Record and validate findings
- **Report Generation**: Create and distribute results
- **Quality Control**: Ensure accuracy and compliance
- **Billing Integration**: Capture charges for tests performed

## LIS vs LIMS: Understanding the Difference

| Feature | LIS (Clinical Labs) | LIMS (Research Labs) |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Primary Focus | Patient diagnostics | Research & QC |
| Data Type | Patient-linked results | Sample-centric data |
| Workflow | Clinical pathways | Research protocols |
| Integration | EHR/HIS systems | Research instruments |
| Compliance | CLIA, CAP, HIPAA | GLP, GMP, FDA 21 CFR |
| Users | Healthcare providers | Scientists, researchers |

**LIS** is designed for clinical diagnostic laboratories serving patient care, while **LIMS** (Laboratory Information Management System) serves research, industrial, and quality control laboratories.

## Why LIS Matters for Healthcare

- **Error Reduction**: Eliminate manual transcription errors
- **Specimen Identification**: Barcode tracking prevents mix-ups
- **Critical Value Alerts**: Immediate notification of dangerous results

Modern clinical labs must comply with:
