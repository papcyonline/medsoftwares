## What is Medication Therapy Management (MTM)?

Medication Therapy Management is a distinct service or group of services provided by pharmacists to optimize therapeutic outcomes for individual patients. MTM services are independent of, but can occur in conjunction with, the provision of a medication product.

### Core MTM Services

**Comprehensive Medication Review (CMR):**
- Systematic review of all patient medications (prescription, OTC, herbals, supplements)
- Identification of medication-related problems
- Development of Personal Medication Record (PMR)
- Creation of Medication Action Plan (MAP)

**Targeted Medication Reviews (TMRs):**
- Focused interventions for specific drug therapy problems
- Adherence assessments and interventions
- Cost-saving opportunities identification

**Additional Clinical Services:**
- Immunization coordination and administration
- Disease state management (diabetes, hypertension, asthma)
- Medication synchronization programs
- Transitions of care services

## 2026 CMS MTM Program Requirements

For 2026, Medicare Part D MTM programs have specific eligibility criteria:

### Patient Eligibility Criteria

Patients qualify for MTM services if they meet ALL three criteria:

**1. Multiple Chronic Conditions:** At least three chronic diseases including:
- Diabetes mellitus
- Chronic heart failure
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia
- Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
- Asthma
- End-stage renal disease (ESRD)
- Mental health conditions

**2. Multiple Part D Drugs:** Taking 8 or more covered Part D maintenance medications concurrently

**3. Cost Threshold:** Likely to incur annual Part D drug costs of $1,276 or more (2026 threshold based on average cost of eight generic drugs)

### MTM Program Deliverables

CMS requires Part D plans to provide:

- **Annual Comprehensive Medication Review (CMR):** In-person, telehealth, or telephonic review
- **Quarterly Targeted Medication Reviews:** Four times per year minimum
- **Personal Medication Record (PMR):** Comprehensive list of all medications
- **Medication Action Plan (MAP):** Patient-friendly summary of recommendations
- **Standardized Format:** CMS-required template for PMR and MAP
- **Intervention Documentation:** Outcomes and recommendations tracking

## Building a Profitable MTM Practice

### Revenue Streams for MTM Services

**1. Medicare Part D MTM Programs**
- Average reimbursement: $50-$75 per CMR
- Volume: Based on eligible patient population
- Payment timeline: Typically quarterly

**2. CPT Code Billing**
- **99605:** Initial 15 minutes, face-to-face
- **99606:** Initial 15 minutes, non-face-to-face
- **99607:** Each additional 15 minutes
- Reimbursement varies by payer ($20-$60 per 15 minutes)

**3. Commercial Payer Contracts**
- Employer-sponsored programs
- Health plan MTM initiatives
- ACO and value-based contracts
- Average: $40-$100 per encounter

**4. Cash-Pay MTM Services**
- Annual medication review: $75-$150
- Focused consultations: $30-$75
- Disease management programs: $50-$100/month

**5. Manufacturer-Sponsored Programs**
- Patient support services
- Adherence programs
- Specialty medication management
- Typical payment: $25-$75 per intervention

## MTM Documentation Best Practices

### Comprehensive Medication Review Documentation

**Required Elements:**
1. **Patient Demographics:** Name, DOB, contact information
2. **Medication List:** All prescription, OTC, herbals, supplements
3. **Allergies and Adverse Reactions:** Complete history
4. **Medical Conditions:** Current diagnoses
5. **Medication-Related Problems Identified:**
   - Indication without medication
   - Medication without indication
   - Ineffective medication
   - Dosage too low
   - Adverse drug reaction
   - Dosage too high
   - Non-adherence
   - Drug interaction
   - Duplicate therapy
   - Cost-related issues

6. **Interventions and Recommendations:**
   - Specific actions taken
   - Provider communications
   - Patient education provided
   - Referrals made

7. **Follow-Up Plan:** Next steps and timeline

8. **Personal Medication Record (PMR):** CMS-required format

9. **Medication Action Plan (MAP):** Patient-friendly summary

### SOAP Note Template for MTM

**Subjective:**
- Chief complaint/reason for visit

- Medication-taking behaviors
- Barriers to adherence

**Objective:**
- Vital signs (if obtained)
- Lab values (if available)
- Medication list verification
- Prescription fill history

**Assessment:**
- Medication-related problems identified
- Clinical status evaluation
- Risk assessment

**Plan:**
- Recommendations for prescribers
- Patient education provided
- Lifestyle modifications discussed
- Follow-up schedule
- Measurable goals established

## Overcoming Common MTM Implementation Challenges

### Challenge 1: Low Patient Participation Rates

**Problem:** Eligible patients don't schedule or complete MTM appointments

**Solutions:**
- **Personal Outreach:** Phone calls more effective than letters
- **Scheduling at Pickup:** Offer MTM when patient picks up prescription
- **Incentives:** Gift cards, medication synchronization, discount programs
- **Convenience:** Evening and weekend appointments, telehealth options
- **Education:** Explain MTM value proposition clearly
- **Multiple Touchpoints:** Combine phone, SMS, email, and in-person invitations

### Challenge 2: Time-Intensive Service Delivery

**Problem:** CMRs taking too long to be profitable

**Solutions:**
- **Standardized Workflows:** Templates and checklists for efficiency
- **Pre-Visit Preparation:** Review medication list before appointment
- **Technology:** Voice-to-text documentation, automated data population
- **Focused Interventions:** Prioritize high-impact issues
- **Staff Support:** Pharmacy technicians for data gathering
- **Batch Scheduling:** Group similar patients for workflow efficiency

### Challenge 3: Limited Reimbursement

**Problem:** MTM revenue insufficient to cover costs

**Solutions:**
- **Multiple Revenue Streams:** Combine Part D, CPT codes, cash pay
- **Increase Completion Rates:** More CMRs = more revenue
- **Upsell Services:** Bundle MTM with adherence packaging, immunizations
- **Efficiency Gains:** Reduce per-service time through standardization
- **Value-Based Contracts:** Pursue ACO and quality-based payments
- **Manufacturer Programs:** Supplement with sponsored services

### Challenge 4: Prescriber Engagement

**Problem:** Providers not responding to MTM recommendations

**Solutions:**
- **EHR Integration:** Send recommendations through provider workflow
- **Concise Communication:** Brief, actionable summaries
- **Relationship Building:** Personal outreach to key prescribers
- **Data Sharing:** Share outcomes demonstrating MTM value
- **Collaborative Practice Agreements:** Formalize pharmacist authority
- **Face-to-Face Meetings:** Present MTM program capabilities
