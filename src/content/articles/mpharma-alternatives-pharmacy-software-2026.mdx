## mPharma Overview

mPharma is a Ghana-headquartered health technology company focused on improving access to affordable medications across Africa. Founded in 2013, the platform primarily operates as a pharmaceutical supply chain and financing solution rather than a traditional pharmacy management system.

**What mPharma does well:**

- **Drug Financing**: Helps pharmacies access affordable medication through direct manufacturer partnerships
- **Supply Chain Optimization**: Manages inventory sourcing and vendor relationships
- **Market Presence**: Operates across approximately 9 African countries including Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Rwanda, and Zambia
- **Mutti Brand**: Operates its own pharmacy franchise network

**Where mPharma falls short:**

- **Subscription Model**: Ongoing monthly fees that can add up for smaller pharmacies
- **Limited POS Features**: Primarily a supply chain tool, not a full pharmacy point-of-sale system
- **Internet Dependency**: Cloud-based model requires consistent internet connectivity
- **Geographic Limitations**: Active in only about 9 countries, leaving much of Africa unserved
- **Tied Supply Chain**: Some pharmacists report limited flexibility in choosing alternative suppliers
- **Not a Full Pharmacy System**: Lacks comprehensive dispensing, billing, and patient management modules

## Key Differences Between mPharma and Alternatives

The most important distinction is that mPharma is fundamentally a supply chain and drug financing platform, while alternatives like PharmaPOS are complete pharmacy management systems. If your pharmacy needs full POS functionality, patient records, prescription management, and financial reporting alongside inventory management, you will need to look beyond mPharma.

### Business Model Differences

mPharma operates on a subscription and commission-based model tied to its supply chain services. This means your pharmacy software costs are ongoing and often linked to purchasing through mPharma's network. Alternatives like PharmaPOS use a one-time licensing model, giving pharmacies full ownership of their software without recurring fees.

### Connectivity Requirements

Many African pharmacies face intermittent internet connectivity. mPharma's cloud-first approach can be problematic in areas with unreliable connections. The best alternatives offer robust offline functionality that syncs automatically when connectivity is restored.
