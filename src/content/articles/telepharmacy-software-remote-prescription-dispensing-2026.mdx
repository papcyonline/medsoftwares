## What is Telepharmacy?

Telepharmacy uses telecommunications and information technology to deliver pharmaceutical care to patients remotely when face-to-face contact with a pharmacist is not feasible. Telepharmacy encompasses multiple service models, all enabled by specialized software platforms.

### Types of Telepharmacy Services

**1. Remote Prescription Verification**
- Pharmacist verifies prescriptions from central location
- Technician prepares medication at satellite location
- Live audio-video consultation for patient counseling
- Final check performed remotely before dispensing

**2. Clinical Telepharmacy Services**
- Medication therapy management (MTM) via video
- Chronic disease management consultations
- Medication adherence counseling
- Post-discharge medication reconciliation

**3. Remote Pharmacy Dispensing**
- Automated dispensing machines in clinics, hospitals, rural areas
- Pharmacist supervision through telepharmacy platform
- Real-time prescription processing and verification
- Remote medication storage management

**4. Telephonic Pharmacy Consultations**
- Phone-based medication counseling
- Prescription refill management
- Side effect management
- Drug information services

## 2026 Regulatory Landscape for Telepharmacy

### DEA Controlled Substance Telemedicine Flexibilities

**Current Status (Through December 31, 2026):**
The DEA issued a fourth extension of COVID-19 telemedicine flexibilities for prescribing controlled medications, effective through December 31, 2026. This maintains important patient access during the transition period.

**Key Provisions:**
- Practitioners may prescribe controlled substances via telemedicine without prior in-person evaluation
- Applies to Schedule II-V controlled medications
- Requires DEA registration and state licensure
- Special registration requirements for Schedule II prescriptions

**Post-2026 Outlook:**
Pharmacies should monitor DEA rulemaking for permanent telemedicine controlled substance prescribing regulations expected in 2026-2027.

### State Telepharmacy Regulations

State laws vary significantly regarding telepharmacy operations:

**Permissive States (Full Telepharmacy Authorization):**
- Alaska, California, Idaho, Iowa, Kansas, Montana, North Dakota
- Allow remote prescription verification and dispensing
- Rural or medically underserved area requirements often apply
- Technician supervision via telepharmacy permitted

**Restrictive States (Limited or No Telepharmacy):**
- Some states prohibit remote verification
- Others require pharmacist physical presence for dispensing
- Regulations evolving rapidly—verify current state board rules

**Common State Requirements:**
- **Audio-Video Technology:** Live, two-way communication required (not recorded video)
- **Pharmacist Visibility:** Patient must see pharmacist during consultation
- **Staffing:** Remote pharmacist counts toward state staffing ratios
- **Recordkeeping:** Telepharmacy consultations documented
- **Site Registration:** Remote locations often require separate licenses

**Verify Compliance:**
Always consult your state board of pharmacy for current telepharmacy regulations before implementation.

## Reimbursement for Telepharmacy Services

### Medicare Telepharmacy Coverage

**Covered Services:**
- Medication therapy management (MTM) via telehealth
- Chronic care management services
- Transitional care management
- Remote patient monitoring

**Billing Codes:**
- **99605-99607:** MTM services
- **99490-99491:** Chronic care management
- **99495-99496:** Transitional care management
- Place of service code 02 (Telehealth) or 10 (Telehealth provided at home)

**Requirements:**
- Interactive audio-video communication
- Patient consent for telehealth
- Originating site in rural HPSA or outside MSA (for some services)
- Pharmacist practitioner status (varies by state)

### Commercial Payer Reimbursement

**Coverage Varies:**
- Many commercial payers cover telehealth pharmacy services
- Payment parity laws in many states require equal reimbursement
- Prior authorization may be required
- Check individual payer policies

### Cash-Pay Telepharmacy Services

**Service Pricing:**
- Medication therapy management: $75-$150 per session
- Disease management consultations: $50-$100
- Prescription delivery fees: $5-$15
- Convenience fees for after-hours services
