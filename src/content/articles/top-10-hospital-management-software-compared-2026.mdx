## 1. Epic Systems

**Best for:** Large health systems, academic medical centers (primarily US)

### Overview:

Epic is the largest EHR/HMS vendor in the United States, serving over 305 million patient records. Known for comprehensive functionality and interoperability.

### Key Features:

- Complete EHR with all clinical modules
- Revenue cycle management
- Population health management
- Patient portal (MyChart)
- Analytics and reporting
- Interoperability (Care Everywhere network)

### Pros:

- Industry-leading functionality
- Excellent interoperability
- Strong patient engagement tools
- Comprehensive training (Epic University)
- Large user community

### Cons:

- Very expensive ($500K-$1M+ implementation)
- Long implementation (12-24 months)
- Primarily US-focused
- Requires significant IT resources
- Complex customization

### Pricing:

- Implementation: $500,000 - $1,000,000+
- Annual maintenance: 15-20% of license
- Per-user licensing models available
- Cloud subscription emerging

### Best For:

Large health systems with 500+ beds, academic medical centers, multi-hospital networks with significant IT budgets and resources.

## Choosing the Right Software

### Decision Framework:

**Consider Your Facility Size:**
- **Large (500+ beds):** Epic, Oracle Health
- **Medium (100-500 beds):** MEDITECH, Oracle Health
- **Small (<100 beds):** HospitalOS, eClinicalWorks, CureMD

**Consider Your Budget:**
- **$500K+:** Epic, Oracle Health
- **$100K-$500K:** MEDITECH, Allscripts
- **Under $50K:** HospitalOS, eClinicalWorks, CureMD
- **Near Zero:** OpenMRS, Bahmni

**Consider Your Region:**
- **United States:** Epic, Oracle, MEDITECH
- **Africa/Middle East:** HospitalOS, Bahmni, OpenMRS
- **Asia:** Oracle Health, HospitalOS
- **Europe:** Oracle Health, Epic (expanding)

**Consider Internet Reliability:**
- **Always connected:** Any cloud solution
- **Unreliable internet:** HospitalOS, on-premise options
- **No internet:** HospitalOS (offline mode)

### When to Choose Each:

**Choose Epic if:**
- You're a large US health system
- You need comprehensive interoperability
- You have $500K+ budget
- You want industry-leading features
- You have strong IT team

**Choose Oracle Health if:**
- You want cloud-first architecture
- You need global deployment
- You're a large hospital system
- You use Oracle products already
- You have government contracts

**Choose HospitalOS if:**
- You're in Africa, Middle East, or Asia
- Internet connectivity is unreliable
- Budget is limited (under $10,000)
- You need quick implementation
- You want no recurring fees

**Choose eClinicalWorks if:**
- You're a small US practice
- Budget is limited
- You want cloud-based solution
- Telehealth is important
- You don't need inpatient features

**Choose OpenMRS/Bahmni if:**
- You have technical resources
- Budget is minimal
- You want open-source flexibility
- Research/data is priority
- You have implementation support

## Conclusion

The best hospital management software depends on your specific needs:

- **For large US health systems:** Epic and Oracle Health lead with comprehensive features, though at premium prices.
- **For mid-size hospitals:** MEDITECH and Allscripts offer strong functionality at lower costs.
- **For developing markets:** HospitalOS provides enterprise features at affordable one-time pricing with crucial offline capability.
- **For small practices:** eClinicalWorks and CureMD deliver cloud solutions at accessible price points.
- **For resource-limited settings:** OpenMRS and Bahmni offer free, open-source alternatives.
